Fatebringer (Timelost)

For complete perks, see d2gunsmith.com, light.gg or destinytracker.com.

This is the “Timelost” (or “Adept”) version of this weapon, which drops only by completing the Conflux encounter Challenge in Vault of Glass on Master mode. Once obtained, you can spend Spoils of Conquest for random rolls after defeating Atheon in Master mode. The rolls here are the same as the normal version.

Fatebringer, Timelost Edition:

  • Bonus Third Column Perk: Explosive Payload * Bonus Fourth Column Perk: Firefly * Can slot Adept mods!

“I don’t get it. Then why is my Timelost Fatebringer marked as a PvP roll?”

DIM matches only one roll at a time, based on the order specified in the wishlist. To avoid all Timelost rolls being marked as the best PvE roll (i.e. ignoring all the other possible rolls you might have had), we’ve made it the last roll in the list. Have no fear, you do indeed have a phenomenal PvE roll, no matter what Timelost roll drops 😍

🛠 No Fate but What we Make (pvp)#

Killing Wind is phenomenal. Killing Wind (procs on kill) lasts six seconds and Kill Clip lasts five seconds (procs on reload). The base reload on Fatebringer is 2.81s (thank you d2gunsmith.com) meaning you’ll have the combined benefit for a minimum of 3.2s. The more you buff your reload, the longer the benefit. No, Rewind Rounds does not proc Kill Clip 🙂

♻️ Do It Again (pve)#

Rewind Rounds returns approximately 40% of the hits you made with it to the magazine recursively. So a Fatebringer with 10 in the magazine will go 10 -> 4 -> 1 = 15 rounds before needing to reload. Fatebringer is a 140 RPM, which equates to 2.3 bullets per second. Kill Clip lasts six seconds which would be or 13.8 (14) bullets. With Rewind Rounds you can make maximum use of Kill Clip without reloading. This of course, assumes you’re hitting every shot 😬😬😬

  • Collector’s Edition Roll
    • Barrels: Hammer-Forged Rifling
    • Magazines: Accurized Rounds
    • Perks 1: Rewind Rounds
    • Perks 2: Kill Clip
    • Masterworks: Range MW
  • Extended Perks (referred to with a ‘+’ below)
    • Barrels: Hammer-Forged Rifling, Smallbore, Corkscrew Rifling, Full Bore, Extended Barrel
