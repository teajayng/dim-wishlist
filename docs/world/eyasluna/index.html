Eyasluna

For complete perks, see d2gunsmith.com, light.gg or destinytracker.com.

Eyasluna is a 140 RPM Legendary Stasis Hand Cannon in the Kinetic slot. It’s available from the final encounter in the Grasp of Avarice Dungeon, added to Destiny in December 2021. It was originally available, and much sought after, in Destiny 1 often referred to as a “mini Hawkmoon” not only because of the look but the name itself. “Luna”… well that’s an exercise for the reader ğŸ˜‰

Eyas: A young hawk, especially (in falconry) an unfledged nestling taken from the nest for training. - lexico.com

It’s a phenomenal weapon with multiple excellent perk pairings and readily available from farming the Grasp boss. I was able to obtain thirty rolls in the first week that it was available.

PvP Thoughts#

With how much hype there is surrounding Eyasluna there’s no shortage of (often-conflicting) content. This is a Good Thing(tm) because it means in this case, Bungie is doing a great job producing perks where, in spite of what many will tell you, there is no clear winning combo for all players and all play styles. There’s two different perspectives I’d suggesting watching/listening to:

  • Eyasluna is ELITE and the God Roll isn’t what others say it is (FULL REVIEW) - CoolGuy
  • Is Eyasluna’s God Roll a Disappointment? - Drewsky

…and a whole bunch of others that you can have on when you’re driving or otherwise looking for background Destiny talk ğŸ™ƒ This list will undoubtedly grow over the coming weeks as people have a chance to get their hands on varying rolls for testing.

  • Eyasluna GOD ROLLS Review (MEH) - Aztecross
  • Destiny 2 - The TWO TAP Eyasluna Is INSANE! - Destiny Fun Police
  • Eyasluna, Fatebringer, Palindrome: Which one is King of the Crucible? - Gleauxx
  • Eyasluna - Better Than Palindrome? - TrueVanguard
  • The Secret God Roll for Eyasluna in Destiny 2 PVP - TrueVanguard

The decision tree for me comes down to one thing: Rangefinder. Rangefinder increases the Zoom factor and if you’re OK with that change in perspective, then you should strongly consider running Rangefinder. If you equip a roll with Rangefinder and find that it feels “off” then well, it’s not for you and that’s 100% OK. If it doesn’t feel good, don’t do it!. You’re not missing out on a make-or-break experience for using Eyasluna. Even if Rangefinder feels great, but you want to do something different and give Perpetual Motion a shot? Well by all means!

PvE Thoughts#

With Headstone, Kill Clip, Demolitionist and Harmony, Eyasluna has a terrific selection of fourth column perks for PvE. There isn’t anything brand new / game-changing so not much to discuss here in the comments section. Perpetual Motion in the third column is the new perk, but I wouldn’t reccomend it in PvP over nearly anything else in the column ğŸ™ƒ

Aztecross points out something worth considering: Headstone is what makes Eyasluna unique from a PvE perspective. Sure it can roll with good combos, but isn’t Fatebringer w/Explosive Rounds and Firefly hard to beat? Or a Judgment with Demolitionist and Adrenaline Junkie? Or generating Warmind Cells with the Seventh Seraph Officer Revolver? Or an Adept Palindrome with Subsistence and Rampage? Run it for a reason, and that reason is Headstone since that’s what makes it unique among 140s.
